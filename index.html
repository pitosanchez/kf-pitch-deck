KIDNEY FORWARD

A NATIONAL INITIATIVE PRIORITIZING KIDNEY HEALTH IN THE UNITED STATES THROUGH LIVING DONATION AWARENESS

At Kidney Forward, our goal is to inspire individuals nationwide to consider donating a kidney, a powerful act that can save lives. Every year, over 100,000 people in the United States await kidney transplants, with many unable to receive one due to the limited availability of kidneys. Through compelling advocacy, innovative education, and strong community engagement, Kidney Forward aims to foster widespread awareness and motivate greater participation in live kidney donation. We strive to build supportive communities and equip individuals with the knowledge and resources they need, ultimately transforming lives through the gift of kidney donation.

Andrew's Journey

A personal story of resilience and hope in the face of kidney disease, inspiring others to take control of their health journey. After receiving a kidney donation from his mother at just 13 years old, Andrew Luu committed himself to maximizing the opportunity afforded by his renewed health. Driven by gratitude and a passion for meaningful impact, he pursued a life dedicated to public service and purposeful endeavors. Andrew began his professional journey in corporate finance but quickly realized it lacked the fulfillment he had experienced running a mentorship program he co-founded during his college years. Deciding to shift gears, Andrew co-founded a social enterprise incubator. Over the next several years, he took on diverse roles, including managing a rapper, producing a feature film, interning for a Congressman, and contributing to the early stages of a start-up that evolved into a prominent e-bike brand. His dedication to community impact led him back to the nonprofit sector, joining Experian's Corporate Social Responsibility team. There, Andrew effectively managed corporate matching programs and spearheaded volunteer initiatives. He subsequently transitioned to the federal government, where he managed various international affairs programs. Committed to serving his local community, Andrew volunteered and served on the Board of Trustees for a local fire department in Virginia. He was also a member of the Recruitment and Retention Committee for the Fairfax County Volunteer Fire and Rescue Association. Andrew holds degrees in Biological Sciences and Economics from the University of California, Irvine, and earned his MBA from Columbia University.

Robert's Experience

Robert joined Kidney Forward following the establishment of his own nonprofit initiative. Driven by his personal journey with chronic kidney disease and extensive firsthand experience navigating the complexities of organ transplantation, Robert is committed to enhancing access to kidney transplants and promoting living kidney donation. Robert's path to advocacy began unexpectedly at age 18, when he was arrested in connection with drugs found in a friend's apartment. Despite having no prior criminal record, no drugs on his person, and no recorded involvement in drug sales, he was sentenced under the severe Rockefeller Drug Laws to a mandatory minimum sentence of 15 years to life. During his incarceration, Robert pursued education as a tool for empowerment, earning a Bachelor's degree in Behavioral Science and a Master's degree in Theology from the New York Theological Seminary. He also co-founded Rehabilitation Through The Arts (RTA), a groundbreaking program offering incarcerated individuals avenues for artistic and creative expression. Now operating for over 25 years across multiple prisons in New York State, RTA's transformative impact inspired the 2024 A24 feature film, "Sing Sing." Upon his release, Robert pursued further education in clinical social work at the Silberman School of Social Work. However, kidney failure forced him to pause his studies. Diagnosed with chronic kidney disease in 2007, Robert began dialysis immediately. A donated kidney from a friend in 2009 lasted five years before failing, requiring another prolonged period of dialysis. Finally, in October 2020, Robert received his second kidney transplant. Motivated by these profound healthcare challenges, Robert founded The Jentosy Project, dedicated to increasing living donor matches within underserved communities and assisting individuals in navigating the organ transplant process. Robert currently resides in New York City, where he channels his creativity as a poet, illustrator, and painter. He enjoys sports, music, and spending meaningful time with friends.

ABOUT KIDNEY FORWARD

Founded in 2024 by Andrew Luu, Kidney Forward is driven by deeply personal experiences with chronic kidney disease and the complexities surrounding kidney transplantation. Andrew received his first kidney transplant at age 13 from his mother—a gift that allowed him to experience two decades of vibrant life. However, by 2020, Andrew's kidney function had deteriorated significantly, initiating the difficult process of seeking another transplant. Encouraged by his extended family's commitment to become living donors, Andrew relocated back to Southern California after spending nearly a decade living on the East Coast and overseas. During this challenging transition, he worked full-time and pursued a Master's degree, strategically positioning himself for reliable health insurance coverage in anticipation of his declining health. Tragically, when the time came, Andrew’s family withdrew their support, leaving him on a waitlist in California, a state where the average wait for a deceased donor kidney ranges from eight to ten years. This painful setback reinforced Andrew’s understanding of living with a chronic, invisible illness—the isolation, the disappointment, and the frustration of navigating a world often unaware of its privilege of health. Today, Kidney Forward is passionately committed to eliminating kidney transplant waitlists by activating living kidney donors at scale. With 18 people dying each day in the United States waiting for a kidney from a deceased donor, our goal is urgent and clear: mobilize latent living donors to ensure no one feels abandoned or alone in their journey towards transplant. What sets Kidney Forward apart is our proactive, hands-on approach. While educational resources are essential, we actively participate in the process, helping individuals identify and connect with potential living donors. This unique participatory model empowers patients and significantly improves patient outcomes, survival rates, and reduces healthcare costs for patients, families, and taxpayers. In 2024, Robert Sanchez joined Kidney Forward, bringing with him extensive experience in advocacy and community engagement, particularly within underserved communities. Robert's own experience as a kidney transplant recipient and his leadership through initiatives like The Jentosy Project significantly enhance Kidney Forward’s mission by addressing disparities and promoting equitable access to transplants for all, irrespective of their background. Kidney Forward remains deeply engaged with each patient's health journey, advocating, supporting, and actively participating to ensure no one navigates this critical process alone. Andrew’s own journey, marked by both setbacks and resilience, inspires Kidney Forward’s unwavering commitment to ensuring that everyone has the opportunity to experience life to its fullest.

COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT AND OUTREACH

Kidney Forward's engagement strategy integrates direct support and community outreach through various channels, including social media, local television, radio broadcasts, print media, public speaking events, and active collaboration with partner organizations.

Faith Institutions

Engaging influential faith-based organizations to spread awareness and encourage community participation in organ donation.

Educational Institutions

Promoting organ donation awareness among students, nurturing the next generation's commitment to healthcare advocacy.

Health-focused Events

Targeting audiences already invested in wellness, offering a natural environment for meaningful dialogue on living kidney donation.

Barbershops and Local Businesses

Capitalizing on trusted community settings to facilitate open, informal conversations about kidney health.
